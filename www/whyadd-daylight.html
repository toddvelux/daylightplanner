Why add daylight?

Why daylight?


Skylights are the most efficient way to bring daylight into a home

  • Brighten dark rooms
  • Balances light in a room
  • Penetrate light into a large rooms

Why daylight?


Danish Building Institute Study
Skylights provide twice as much light as vertical windows.
Skylights provide daylight to the core of the space.
Better quality and balance of light, less glare.
Smaller square footage of glass required.

  • Dormer
    0.85% Daylight factor

  • Window
    1.41% Daylight factor

  • Skylight
    2.86% Daylight factor

Why daylight?


Research shows:

  • Skylights generate higher lighting output than windows.*
  • When skylights are used appropriately in combination with vertical windows, heating and cooling loads savings can be generated 98% of the time compared to designs without skylights.**
  • Skylights can help reduce square footage of glazing in the building envelope by up to 60% while achieving a target daylight factor.**
  • Proper combination of skylights and windows can help reduce the solar heat gain in the building envelope by 60%+.***
*** 2009 Danish Building Institute Study
*** 2012 Group 14 Study of the Impact of Skylights on the buiulding envelope
*** 2012 Renssalaer Polytchnic Institute Lighting Research Center Study

Why daylight?


Practical:

  • Where there is light, there is life.
  • Brighten a dark room.
  • Add light and privacy to a bathroom.
  • Provides white healthy daylight versus yellow light bulbs.

Why daylight?


Dramatic

  • No single remodeling project can change a room like adding natural light.
  • A focal point to a room.
  • Lowest cost home remodel with the most impact.
  • Make the volume of the room increase and appear larger.

Why daylight?


Necessary

  • Improves the health of our families.
  • Children learn faster under daylight.
  • 20% of the population suffers from SAD (Seasonal Affective Disorder).
  • Elderly people need more light to prevent falls.
